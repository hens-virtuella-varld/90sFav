Intro

The origins of the Internet date back to a project under the United States Department of Defense in the 60s.
The purpose was to enable users to share computing resources at the same time.

In the 1970s, the network served regional academic and military networks.

Then, the Internet was widely used by academia in the 80s.
Meanwhile, worldwide development and the merger of networks happened during this period due to contributions from national funding as well as private commercial funds.

By the early 1990s, the linking of commercial networks and enterprises marked the beginning of the transition to the modern Internet.
Gradually, the commercialisation incorporated Internet services and technologies into virtually every aspect of modern life.
